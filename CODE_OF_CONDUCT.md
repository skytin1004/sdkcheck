# Code of Conduct

sdkcheck aims to be a useful, direct, and respectful open-source project.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful when disagreeing.
- Critique code, docs, decisions, and trade-offs rather than people.
- Assume contributors may be working across different time zones, languages, and levels of familiarity with Rust or CI tooling.
- Keep discussions focused on making sdkcheck safer, clearer, and more useful.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks.
- Repeated off-topic disruption.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Sharing credentials, private logs, or sensitive customer data in issues, PRs, or discussions.

## Reporting

For security issues, use [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

For conduct concerns, contact the project maintainers through [github.com/skytin1004/sdkcheck](https://github.com/skytin1004/sdkcheck). Until a dedicated contact path is published, raise the issue directly with the maintainer who invited the contribution.

Maintainers may remove comments, close discussions, or restrict participation when behavior makes collaboration unsafe or unproductive.
